Why You Should Be More like Halloumi Cheese

Author: Alysa Zhao

Date: Jul 7 2025

We all know and love gooey Mozzarella, salty and zingy Parmesan, and even smokey Gouda. These cheeses' ability to melt beautifully is central to their charm, turning pizzas into pizz-ahhs and pastas into creamy luxuries.

But that's predictable, boring even. Cheese pulls and melts are beloved, but perhaps getting old.

Then there is a different kind of cheese, one that resists surrender to temperature. Halloumi, a fresh cheese originating from Cyprus and traditionally made with sheep’s, goat’s, and sometimes cow’s milk, is an outlier in the cheese world. Thanks to a second heating in hot whey, its protein structure is tightened, allowing it to resist changes in form, flavor, and character under intense broiling or grilling.

Unlike its melty counterparts, halloumi develops a crisp, golden edge while remaining chewy inside. Where others have long collapsed into puddle (albeit a delicious one), halloumi stands its ground and becomes even more remarkable when tested by fire.

There’s something admirable about a cheese like that: one that is tempered stronger through heat. In a world that often urges us to blend in or soften under a majority, maybe there’s something we can learn from halloumi: when things get heated, stay true to your character. You might just shine even brighter!
